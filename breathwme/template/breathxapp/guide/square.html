The Square

A symbol of balance, stability, and control — the square is often used as a guide for breathing exercises known as "box breathing" or "square breathing." It helps calm the mind, reduce stress, and bring a sense of structure and relaxation by evenly pacing your breaths.

What is Square Breathing?

Square breathing, also called box breathing, is a simple technique where each side of the square represents a part of your breath cycle: inhale, hold, exhale, hold. Each step is timed evenly, promoting calm and focus.

How It Works:
  • Inhale - Breathe in for 4 seconds
  • Hold - Hold your breath for 4 seconds
  • Exhale - Slowly breathe out for 4 seconds
  • Hold - Pause again for 4 seconds

Benefits of Square Breathing

  • Reduces Stress: Activates the parasympathetic nervous system to calm your body.
  • Improves Focus: Encourages mindfulness and present-moment awareness.
  • Balances the Breath: Each phase of breathing is equally timed.
  • Enhances Performance: Used by athletes, military, and professionals to regain control under pressure.
  • Easy to Practice: Can be done anywhere, anytime without special equipment.
Did You Know?

The Navy SEALs use square breathing before high-pressure missions to stay sharp and focused. It’s also widely adopted in yoga, therapy, and meditation practices.

Where It's Used

Yoga & Meditation

Square breathing is integrated into many yoga and meditation practices to enhance awareness and breath control.

Therapy & Counseling

Used by therapists to help patients regulate emotions and manage anxiety or panic attacks.

Military & High-Performance Fields

Professionals use it to prepare for high-stress situations, maintain focus, and stay calm under pressure.
