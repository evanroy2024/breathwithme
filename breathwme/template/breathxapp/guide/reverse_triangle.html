The Reverse Triangle

The reverse triangle turns the usual pattern upside down. Instead of inhale-hold-exhale, you begin with **exhale**, then **hold**, and finish with **inhale**. It symbolizes **letting go first**, releasing what you don’t need, and then gently bringing in calm. Ideal for **releasing stress**, quieting the mind, and promoting relaxation.

What is Reverse Triangle Breathing?

This breathing method starts with a deep **exhale** — letting go of stress — followed by a pause, and then a slow, calm **inhale**. Each step lasts for the same count, forming an inverted triangle in your mind.

Example Pattern (4-4-4):
  • Exhale for 4 seconds
  • Hold for 4 seconds
  • Inhale for 4 seconds

Visualize the triangle flipped — down, across, and up — symbolizing release, stillness, and renewal.

Why It Works

  • Encourages Release: Starts with exhalation to let go of tension and overthinking.
  • Promotes Calm: The reversed pattern slows the breath and eases nervous energy.
  • Grounds the Body: Helps anchor you in the present moment through mindful breathing.
  • Good for Sleep: Ideal before bed or during restless moments.
Quick Tip

Think of your breath like water draining from the top of a triangle — down, pause, and gently refill.
