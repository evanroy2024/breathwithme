The Circle

A visual and interactive guide for deep breathing exercises. This animated circle helps you synchronize your breath—inhale, hold, and exhale—while promoting mindfulness and relaxation through a calming visual rhythm.

What It Does

This circle isn't just a shape—it's a tool for your breathwork. It gently expands to guide your **inhale**, pauses at full size to indicate a **hold**, and then contracts to signal a calm **exhale**.

The visual animation is based on BPM (Breaths Per Minute), typically set between 4–6 for a relaxing rhythm. This promotes deep diaphragmatic breathing and helps regulate your nervous system.

Breathing Pattern:
  • Inhale: Circle expands smoothly (e.g., 4 seconds)
  • Hold: Circle stays full (e.g., 2 seconds)
  • Exhale: Circle contracts back (e.g., 6 seconds)

Benefits

  • Reduces Anxiety: Helps calm your mind and lower stress levels
  • Improves Focus: Brings attention back to the present moment
  • Boosts Mood: Promotes the release of calming neurotransmitters
  • Lowers Heart Rate: Slows breathing and promotes a parasympathetic state
  • Simple & Effective: Anyone can do it, anytime, anywhere
Pro Tip:

Try 4-7-8 breathing with the circle: Inhale for 4 seconds, hold for 7, and exhale for 8. Repeat for 4 cycles to feel immediate relaxation.

How It Works

Animated Rhythm

The circle animation is synced with the inhale and exhale timing. As the shape expands, you breathe in; as it contracts, you breathe out. This visual guidance helps establish a healthy, natural breathing pace.

Example Cycle:

Inhale (4s) → Hold (2s) → Exhale (6s)

Total Cycle: 12 seconds → 5 BPM

Why Visual Guidance Helps

Many people unknowingly breathe too fast or shallow. This tool retrains your breathing rhythm using a calm visual anchor. It’s especially effective for meditation, stress relief, and sleep preparation.

Use Case:

Try it before a big meeting, after a workout, or while winding down for bed. Even just 60 seconds can bring noticeable calmness.

Pro Tips for Best Experience

  • Use headphones and calming background music to enhance focus
  • Sit comfortably with your spine upright
  • Let your belly rise and fall naturally—avoid shallow chest breathing
  • Set a timer or follow the on-screen guidance for 2–5 minutes daily
  • Repeat multiple times a day to train your body to stay calm
