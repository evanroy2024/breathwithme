The Triangle

The triangle represents **balance and structure** — a strong foundation for breathing. Triangle breathing uses equal parts of inhale, hold, and exhale, forming a mental triangle that brings focus and calm. It’s great for **reducing anxiety**, improving **mental clarity**, and helping you feel centered.

What is Triangle Breathing?

Triangle breathing follows a **3-part pattern** — inhale, hold, exhale — all for the same amount of time. It’s often used to calm the nervous system and sharpen focus.

Example Pattern (4-4-4):
  • Inhale for 4 seconds
  • Hold for 4 seconds
  • Exhale for 4 seconds

Visualize each side of a triangle as you breathe — up, across, and down.

Why It Works

  • Reduces Stress: Slows your heart rate and calms the mind.
  • Enhances Focus: Helps in staying present and aware.
  • Builds Balance: The equal pattern fosters inner stability and control.
  • Easy to Remember: Simple 3-part cycle — great for quick grounding.
Quick Tip

Visualize a triangle forming in your mind — breathe in along one side, hold across the top, exhale down the other side.

Where It's Used

Anxiety Relief

Used in cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) and mindfulness to manage anxious thoughts.

Work & Study Focus

A quick reset tool before exams, meetings, or problem-solving sessions.

Daily Mindfulness

Just 3–5 minutes a day can improve emotional regulation and peace of mind.
