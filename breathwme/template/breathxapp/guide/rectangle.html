The Rectangle

The rectangle represents a breathing rhythm where inhaling and exhaling are different lengths — a calming technique ideal for relaxation, sleep, and anxiety control. Just like its shape, one side is longer, helping you slow down and settle your mind with every breath.

What is Rectangle Breathing?

Rectangle breathing is a technique where you breathe in for a short count and exhale for a longer one — similar to how a rectangle has different side lengths. This method calms the nervous system and is especially effective for sleep and stress relief.

A Common Pattern:
  • Inhale - 4 seconds
  • Exhale - 6 to 8 seconds

Repeat this cycle slowly, following the length of each “side” of the rectangle with your breath.

Why It Works

  • Relieves Stress: Longer exhalations help activate your parasympathetic nervous system — the “rest and digest” state.
  • Improves Sleep: Slows heart rate and calms the body before bed.
  • Balances Emotions: A great tool to manage anxiety, frustration, or overwhelm.
  • Simple and Effective: No tools needed — just your breath and a bit of focus.
Quick Tip

If you ever feel anxious in public, try rectangle breathing quietly. Count in your head — in for 4, out for 6 — and you’ll feel a wave of calm in just a minute.

Visual Breathing Guide

Watch the rectangle stretch and shrink as a guide for your breath. Inhale with the short side, exhale with the long side. Let your breathing follow its pace.

Where It's Used

Sleep Preparation

Practiced before bed, rectangle breathing helps ease you into a restful state and improves sleep quality.

Anxiety Relief

Used in therapy and wellness programs to manage anxiety and lower stress levels through longer exhales.

Office & Daily Calm

Even at your desk, a few rounds of rectangle breathing can refresh your focus and mood instantly.

Try It Now

Complete 6 cycles of rectangle breathing. Inhale for 4 seconds, exhale for 6 or 8 seconds. As you breathe, picture each breath traveling along the sides of a rectangle: short side in, long side out.
